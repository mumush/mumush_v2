Always Close Your Tags

Many people may forget to leave off the closing tags. By today's standards, this is simply bad practice and should be 100% avoided. Always, always close your tags. Otherwise, you'll encounter validation and glitch issues at every turn.

Organize Your HTML5

It always makes sense to lay your stylesheet out in a way that allows you to quickly find parts of your code. I recommend a top-down format that tackles styles as they appear in the source code. See the following link for more information on HTML/CSS structure.

Containers

Use a div tag to wrap your content appropriately. It is better to have control of your content as well as flexible to your design. Additionally, don't forget to add an ID or a class to your wrapper. It's the best practice to use most of these tags.

Always Comment your Code

HTML files can become quite extensive, spanning hundreds of lines. These large files can make finding and editing code nearly impossible. Use comments to build a table of contents at the top of your file, as well as outline blocks of code throughout the file. Doing so tells others exactly what is contained within the file, whereabout the styles are located, and exactly what a specific style pertains to. Always Validate

Learning to validate your HTML is an important step for most designers. By writing valid HTML you ensure that your pages are standards compliant and will run on the most user agents and web browsers. It's no different than comparing a sentence you write to the rules of grammar for the language you wrote it in. In fact the grammar analogy is a good one to keep in mind when you think of validating your code.
